Where to go?

Marmaris
Turkey

A gorgeous backdrop of pine-clad mountains looms behind the seaside resort of Marmaris in southwest Turkey. It’s known for buzzing nightlife on Bar Street, which is home to open-air clubs and music venues. Marmaris offers an abundance of fast food restaurants, the restored castle of Suleyman the Magnificent and an appealing cobblestoned old quarter.

The town also makes a good base for gület cruises, diving in pristine waters and visiting the Greek isle of Rhodes. Outside the busy town, coves and bays of the Datça Peninsula make lovely daytrip destinations for those travelling by boat. It is also a centre for sailing and diving, possessing two major and several smaller marinas.

The Marmaris Castle is one of the few castles in Turkey that also possesses a museum

Fantastic views from small boats to yachts worth a fortune
